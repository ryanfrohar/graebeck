Montfort Hospital
Multi-Area Renovation

The Montfort Hospital renovation project on Montreal Road demonstrates Graebeck Construction's expertise in the most technically demanding segment of institutional construction: active health-care facility renovation with critical infection control requirements and specialized medical infrastructure.

The project encompassed multi-area renovations throughout the hospital, with a primary focus on the Emergency Room. The ER scope included selective demolition of existing spaces, construction of new patient facilities including a modern waiting room designed to reduce patient stress and improve patient flow, and — critically — a lead-lined CT scanner room. The construction of a medically compliant lead-lined room requires extremely precise tolerances, specialized materials, and coordination with radiological physicists to verify shielding effectiveness prior to commissioning.

As with all Graebeck healthcare projects, patient care was maintained throughout. Infection Prevention and Control (IPAC) protocols were strictly observed, including negative pressure environments during demolition, HEPA filtration, and sealed corridor access routes separating the construction zone from active clinical areas at all times.

Technical Highlights

Lead-Lined CT Scanner Room

Construction of the lead-lined room was executed to medical-grade radiation shielding specifications, verified by a radiological physicist before equipment installation and commissioning.

Strict IPAC Protocols

Infection Prevention and Control measures — including negative air pressure zones, HEPA filtration, and sealed access routes — protected patients and staff throughout active construction.

ER Remained Operational

Emergency services continued without interruption. Careful sequencing allowed ER functions to remain active while new patient areas were constructed and commissioned in sequence.
